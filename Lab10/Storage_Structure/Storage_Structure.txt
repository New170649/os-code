Chapter 11: Mass-Storage Systems - Lab 10
Topic 1: Overview of Mass Storage Structure

Q1 (a) Analysis:
The formula (cylinders * tracks_per_cylinder * sectors_per_track * bytes_per_sector)
represents the total raw storage capacity of a Hard Disk Drive (HDD).

Calculation:
1024 cylinders * 16 tracks * 63 sectors * 512 bytes
= 528,482,304 bytes
≈ 504 MB

Q1 (c) Difference between Track and Cylinder:

Track: A single circular ring of data on one surface of one platter.

Cylinder: A vertical stack of all tracks located at the same radial distance
from the center across all surfaces/platters.

Reading an entire cylinder is faster because it requires no mechanical "Seek"
movement, only an electronic switch between the heads.
