Topic 3: NVM Scheduling

Q3 (a) Why NVM does NOT require seek optimization:
NVM (SSD/Flash) has no moving mechanical parts. There is no read/write head
that needs to physically move (seek) across a spinning disk. Access time
is almost uniform for any location (LBA), typically ~0.1ms.
Therefore, complex algorithms like SSTF or SCAN provide no real benefit
over simple FCFS.

Q3 (c) Wear Leveling:
NAND flash cells have a limited lifespan (10k - 100k writes).
Wear Leveling is a technique used by the flash controller to distribute
write operations evenly across all physical blocks.

Simulation Logic:
Instead of writing to the same physical location every time a logical block
is updated, the controller maps the logical address to a physical block
with the lowest "Write Count" to prevent premature wear-out of specific cells.
